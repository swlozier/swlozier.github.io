About


For this assignment, you will work with a partner on a research and design project, distant reading Frankenstein to look for patterns that may reveal information about how gender is working in the novel. This assignment focuses on the Distant Reading text method, but also focuses on gender literary criticism and multimedia essay creation (following the models of the Pudding articles we read this unit).

Part One


With your partner, use AntConc to perform a distant reading analysis of gender in the novel. You may look for any kinds of patterns, but following the models set by the Pudding articles and course discussions, you may consider looking for things like:

  • the kinds activities female characters do relative to male
  • the amount of speaking female characters do relative to male
  • the connotations of adjectives or other words associated with female characters
  • descriptions of female characters, relative to male
  • how much of the text women take up relative to men
  • if different narrators represent or talk about women differently
  • if the temporal development of the text reveals a change in how women are represented
  • settings particularly associated with women (or settings where women show up vs settings where they don't)
While you should let the research take you where it needs to go, your essay will ultimately need to demonstrate at least 3 patterns of gender in the novel. You will also need to save the raw data and AntConc output that illustrates these patterns.

Part Two


Using Twine as your primary platform, you and your partner will design and write a multimedia essay to represent your findings about gender in Frankenstein using Antconc. These essays should follow the models set by the distant reading essays we have read during this unit, however they will likely be less polished in presentation given time and resources (this is fine!).

It should, however include the following:

  • creative and useful data visualization of the patterns you uncover in Antconc
  • Text that introduces the research question, methodology, and explains the information visualized in the data (again -- see the examples for how to do this)
  • An attention to twine's interactions, design, and linking structures to present your essay
  • An attention to the overall design (you should plan to do custom CSS or use more complex aspects of Twine's programming) of your essay
  • links to your raw data output from Antconc, and any other platforms / tools you use to analyze your data
  • A clear introduction and conclusion

Submission Information


One partner will submit the final essay as an HTML document (downloaded from Twine) to Moodle by the due date.

Assessment

Your grade will be calculated according to the following breakdown:

CONTENT 60% -- does your essay clearly respond to the research question and prompt, to use distant reading methodologies to analyze the role of gender in Frankenstein? Have you analyzed gender in a way that reflects the unit's focus? Have you performed distant reading in a way that reflects the unit's focus?

STYLE 40% -- does you essay include multimedia elements appropriate to the form? does it include a clear and logical organizational structure? Does it include creative and useful data visualizations? Is it designed beyond Twine's default styling? Is writing clear and free of errors in things like spelling, capitalization, punctuation, idiomatic English grammar?
