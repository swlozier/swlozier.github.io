Week 2: Jan 25-31



MONDAY 1/25

READ: from Sara Wachter-Boettcher, Technically Wrong, Chapter 1 & 3 

Available: Readings*

As you read, think about the following:

  1. What problems of social media interface design does Wachter-Boettcher focus on?
  2. Why do you think tech companies make these mistakes of design that Wachter-Boettcher highlights?
  3. Have you ever been on the receiving end of one of these poor designs, where a tech platform made assumptions about its users, and didn't include you in those assumptions?

*Note: I've requested that the library purchase an ebook version of this text, but that has not happened yet. I've scanned the chapters into the Leganto Reading List and you should be able to see the PDF from there.


WEDNESDAY 1/27

READ:  from Sara Wachter-Boettcher, Technically Wrong , Chapter 4 & 5 

Available: Readings

As you read, think about the following:

  1. Times you have ever filled out a form or something similar, and you have not been represented (eg: not enough spaces for your name; errors due to punctuation in your name; misgendered, etc.)
  2. Time you have experienced the cognitive dissonance by forced "delight" as discussed in chapter 5 OR times you've noticed, and maybe even laughed at, something clever / delightful from a tech communication, similar to what is discussed in chapter 5
