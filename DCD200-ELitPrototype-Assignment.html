About

This is your fifth, and final major assignment in this class, before the final reflection. You have a number of choices for approaching this work, as noted below. As well, you have the week of 4/26 - 5/2 to work on this.

Task

To close our our electronic literature unit, you will each create a work of electronic literature. The type of work is entirely up to you, but you are constrained to one of the four types that we looked at in detail last week:

AUGMENTED REALITY NARRATIVE

Following the model of Between Page and Screen, create a short narrative that uses Augmented Reality to play with text and image. The text must have at least 5 triggers, and you may do a paper prototype (following the example of last unit's assignment) if the technology is not working.

HYPERTEXT NARRATIVE

Following the model of Hunt for the Gay Planet, use Twine to write and design a choice-based narrative. The narrative must have at least 10 moments of choice, and include some advanced programming per the tutorial and in-class Twine work ("if" statements, text styling, etc.).

KINETIC POETRY

Following The Dreamlife of Letters, design and stage a piece of kinetic poetry using Stepworks. You have many options for how to approach this. If you are poetically-inclined, you may write an original piece; you can create a "lyric video" similar to what we did in class to Lubalin's song; you can modify an existing poem to make it kinetic. The piece must be a complete poem, with attention to rhythm and one-click interactions of Stepworks.

TEXT GENERATOR / TWITTER BOT

 Following the numerous twitterbot examples, use Tracery and Cheapbotsdonequick (CBDQ) to create a text generator. You could keep this VERY literary by creating a generator that writes poetry or tells short stories, or you can go more non-traditional and play with emojis, memes, familiar sayings, existing text, or something else. The twitterbot must be presented as part of a fully realized twitter profile (cover / profile images, bio explaining that it is a bot, a good name, etc.) and have at least 5 rules beyond the origin, and ~20 alternatives per rule (to the extent that this makes sense). 


Designer's Statement

In addition to your work of e-lit, you will be responsible for writing a short designer's statement in which you reflect on your designs and discuss them in terms of the following:

  1. What were your narrative and design goals for this project?
  2. How well do you think you achieved each of these goals?
  3. Did you use any of the example texts as inspiration for your work? If so, which ones? If not, where did you draw inspiration for this work?
  4. What was the most challenging part of this project?
  5. If given more time / resources, what would you improve on this mini-project?
