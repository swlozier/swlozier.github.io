About

This is your major assignment to close out unit 3. It is a scaled down, but still relatively hefty assignment that brings together everything we've done in this unit: SVG and images.


Basic Task

Create a single image "comic" that features two tangram animals (drawn with SVG) having a conversation (via SVG text and thought / speech bubbles) in a real place (a photographic image background).
Each comic must feature the following:
  • 2 tangram-style animals drawn with SVG (inspo on Google images; remember each shape you use should be evident in the final product; so think about stroke and fill colors that will allow you to do this)
  • a real photograph for place to create the setting
  • at least one speech or thought bubble for each animal that shows their conversation

Designer's Statement

In addition to creating these three modifications, you will be responsible for writing a short designer's statement in which you reflect on your designs and discuss them in terms of the following:

  1. What were your design goals for this comic?
  2. How well do you think you achieved each of these goals?
  3. What was the most challenging part of this mini-project?
  4. If given more time / resources, what would you improve on this mini-project?

Submission

  1. Zip (Compress) the folder directory that contains your image(s), HTML file, and stylesheet (if you use one). 
  2. Upload that zipped folder here on Moodle.
  3. Submit your designer's statement as a PDF file

Assessment

In assessing this work, I will be looking for the following:

  • 70% -- COMIC: your comic includes all of the requirements listed above, showing your purposeful work with SVG, images, and text toward short-form storytelling
  • 30% -- DESIGNER'S STATEMENT: Your designer's statement addresses each of the questions above and is written with attention the rhetorical and mechanical standards of formal, academic English.
