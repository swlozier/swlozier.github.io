Quick Make: Readers as Writers

For this week's Quick Make, you will work with a tool called Twine -- a freely available platform for building hypertexts through a visual layout logic. It is very easy to get started, but contains LOTS of possibilities for more complex coding or programming or otherwise using the computational / digital space. (We will return to Twine for a few other Quick Makes this semester, because of this).

PROMPT: Using our primary source texts this week for topical inspiration, create a short story in Twine that features a situation where the reader must become a writer. Your story may be about any topic, but must contain the following:

  1. At least 5 passages in Twine, connected to one another (note: these do not necessarily have to be branching or multilinear passages, though they certainly can be). 
  2. At least 2 instances where the reader WRITES part(s) of the narrative. Although choice-based and interactive narrative such as we've been reading this semester introduce the conceptual terrain of the reader-as-writer, for this quick make you should approach the reader-as-writer idea as literal. That is, there should be a text box (or something like it) where the reader can literally WRITE WORDS that will have some effect on the story (as in  First Draft of the Revolution). 

NOTE: This will require more complex coding in Twine that most of you have likely explored. While you will need to figure out how to incorporate a writing feature into the short story you write, you can find the code and explanation in the Harlowe 3.2.2 manual (HINT: check out the input-box and force-input-box macros).

During this (and every) Quick-make, I encourage you to also explore writerly storytelling techniques -- think about story instances where a reader might reasonably need to write something, for instance. As well, if you are familiar with Twine (as I imagine most of you are), I encourage you to try out a technique you have not used before (beyond the requirements for this quick make).

SUBMISSION: Remember that if you are working with the online version of Twine, you CANNOT JUST SHARE THE LINK! When you are done, select PUBLISH TO FILE from the bottom left menu. This will create an HTML file and THAT is what you will submit here.
