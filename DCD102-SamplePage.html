Read Adams & Sagarwala, Web Design Primer, Chapter 1 

Chapter one from our course textbook outlines the basic building blocks of the web, framing this in terms of print. If you have never really thought about print layout, it may be helpful to have a physical book near you (a textbook, encyclopedia, novel, or anything will work) to look at and think about in terms of design & layout.

 As well, the chapter covers some basic features of HTML, CSS, and Javascript which we will be revisiting in Unit 2 (so do not freak out if you can't remember all of it! Just use this time to familiarize yourself with the ideas).


Read "The Anatomy of a Web Site" Article 

This article provides a helpful overview and discussion of effective page layout, and ways to organize information in an online space. This will be useful as we move through this class. It will also be helpful to refer back to for your web page "close reading" assignment. 

Note: there are images included throughout the article that look a little like ads. These are example images that illustrate important design points, so take care when scrolling through.

Complete the Unit 1 Major Assignment, Close Reading Websites 

For this assignment, you will analyze 3 websites in terms of their design. Using the "Anatomy of a Web Site" article and the Voicethread example as your guides, you will take apart and examine the design elements of the sites, to ultimately offer an assessment of how well you think the site is designed, what is working and what isn't.
