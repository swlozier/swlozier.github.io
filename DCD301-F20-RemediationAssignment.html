About

For your final "big" project this semester, you will create your own adaptation/remediation of Frankenstein, following the adaptation and remediation discussions in class. You will primarily use using Inky -- the interactive text language developed by Inkle -- to explore the effects that interaction can have on adapting a narrative.


Task

Select a passage, moment, chapter, or volume from Frankenstein that you would like to adapt in some way. Re-read it carefully, noting things like major themes present, settings, characters, and so on (this is a great place to refer back to your unit 1 map). Re-work that moment to adapt it according to any of the lenses discussed in class:
  • plot adaptation: what happens if the major events happen differently? What happens if you give more time or focus to minor events?
  • cultural adaptation: how does this moment or passage play out differently if set in or responsive to a different cultural moment than Mary Shelley's? How can you maintain some of the major themes or concerns, adapted for a different time?
Once you have determined the focus of your textual adaptation, you will write/create it using Inky, a mark-up program for creating choice-based narratives (similar to Twine, but with a different coding UI and output style). 


Requirements

While the length of your inspiration passage / moment can be anything at all, there are a few requirements for your own output.

  • You must include at least 15 moments of choice in the remediation (20 if collab.);
  • You must include at least 3 points of custom CSS & styling beyond Inky's default (5 if collab.)
  • You must include at least 1 image (3 if collab.)
Note: In addition to the increased quantitative expectations for a collaboration, I will also be looking for a more complete and robust adaptation, in terms of how much of the story is there, how detailed the writing is, how critically and creatively you've approached your adaptation. 

Submission

When you are finished writing your text in inky, you will Export for the Web. This will force-download a folder* (you should recognize it as a web page directory) with your game. You can then:
  • Zip / Compress the folder and upload it directly to Moodle
  • Zip/Compress the folder and upload it to itch.io, then share the itch.io link in Moodle

*This folder is also where you will be able to find your .css file to customize your styling

***Make sure you DO NOT move anything in the folder around, or leave anything out when you compress and submit to Moodle***
